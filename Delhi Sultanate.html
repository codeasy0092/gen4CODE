Introduction

The Delhi Sultanate was a period of five Muslim dynasties that ruled parts of northern India, shaping the region's culture and politics.

Qutub Minar - an iconic structure from the Delhi Sultanate period

Dynasties

The five dynasties of the Sultanate included the Mamluk, Khilji, Tughlaq, Sayyid, and Lodi dynasties.

Legacy

The Delhi Sultanate laid the foundation for Indo-Islamic culture, administration, and architecture in India.
